SECTION 1 — THE CORE IDEA (WHY WE REDESIGNED)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The original design had the form data going directly into a chat.
The new design adds a crucial step in between: a Research Agent that does
deep homework on the client BEFORE the conversation even opens.

This is exactly what every great human consultant does — they research
you before they walk in the door.

OLD FLOW (what we had before):
  Form → Chat opens cold → Agent asks from zero

NEW FLOW (the redesign):
  Form → Research Agent does deep homework → Chat opens with agent
  already knowing real things about the client

The result: The client feels they are talking to a real consultant
who actually prepared — not a generic AI chatbot.

SECTION 3 — THE 4 PARALLEL RESEARCH LANES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All 4 lanes run AT THE SAME TIME (in parallel) to save time.
Total research time: 2-3 minutes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LANE 1 — THE BUSINESS'S OWN DIGITAL FOOTPRINT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Purpose: Find everything the business has online — what exists,
         what's missing, what's active, what's dead.

Queries built automatically from the form data:
  → "[business_name] [city]"
  → "[business_name] Facebook"
  → "[business_name] Instagram"
  → "[business_name] تقييمات / reviews"
  → "[business_name] Google Maps"

What we extract and store:

  Google Maps Listing:
    - Does it exist? (yes/no)
    - Star rating (e.g. 4.3 stars)
    - Number of reviews (e.g. 94 reviews)
    - Number of photos uploaded
    - Is the menu listed?
    - Is delivery enabled?
    - Listing completeness score (how well set up is their profile)

  Facebook Page:
    - Does it exist? (yes/no)
    - Follower count (e.g. 1,240 followers)
    - When was the last post? (e.g. 14 days ago)
    - Posting frequency (daily / weekly / very low / inactive)
    - Average engagement rate
    - What content types they post (photos, videos, offers)
    - What is missing from their page

  Instagram Account:
    - Does it exist? (yes/no)
    - Follower count
    - Last post date
    - Type of content (reels, posts, stories)

  TikTok:
    - Does it exist? (yes/no)
    - Follower count

  Website:
    - Does it exist? (yes/no)
    - Is it functional?
    - Does it have e-commerce?

  Delivery Platforms (Talabat, Elmenus, Noon — for F&B):
    - Is the business listed on any delivery app?

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LANE 2 — COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Purpose: Find who the real competition is in the same area
         and category — with real data about how strong they are.

Queries:
  → "[industry] [neighborhood/city] مصر"
  → "أفضل [industry] في [city]"
  → "[industry] near [location]" (Google Maps category search)
  → "[industry] [city] Instagram"

What we extract for each competitor found:

  For each competitor (top 3-5):
    - Business name
    - Distance from client's location
    - Google Maps rating (vs client's rating)
    - Number of reviews (vs client's)
    - Instagram follower count
    - Facebook follower count
    - Their apparent differentiator (what makes them stand out)
    - Their strengths (what they do better)
    - Their weaknesses (where they fall short)
    - Are they stronger or weaker than the client overall?

  Summary analysis:
    - Overall competitive landscape description
    - What gap exists in the market that the client could fill

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LANE 3 — MARKET & INDUSTRY CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Purpose: Understand what is working RIGHT NOW for this industry
         in Egypt and the MENA region.

Queries:
  → "تسويق [industry] مصر 2025"
  → "[industry] Egypt social media marketing trends"
  → "أفضل استراتيجية تسويق [industry] مصر"
  → "Egypt [industry] market insights"

What we extract:

  - What marketing channels are actually working for this
    industry in Egypt right now (not generic advice)
  - What content formats perform best (Reels vs posts vs stories)
  - Seasonal context (Ramadan? summer? back-to-school period?)
  - Any recent industry news that affects their market
  - Consumer behavior signals for their customer type

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LANE 4 — SOCIAL MEDIA SIGNAL CAPTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Purpose: Find what content and trends are winning on social media
         in their specific niche, in Egypt.

Queries:
  → Hashtag analysis: #[industry]مصر #[city] #[industry]
  → What content is trending in their niche on Instagram/TikTok Egypt
  → What questions people ask about this business type online

What we extract:

  - Trending hashtags in their niche (Egypt-specific)
  - Which content formats competitors use that get high engagement
  - Common customer complaints and praises in the category
  - Any influencer activity in their niche

Why Tavily over raw Google Search?
  Tavily is designed specifically for AI agents. It returns clean,
  structured, LLM-ready results with confidence scores.
  It handles Arabic queries well and is faster and cheaper than
  running a raw browser-based search.

Why Apify over direct Instagram/Facebook APIs?
  The official Instagram and Facebook APIs are heavily restricted.
  They don't allow reading public profiles without complex approval
  processes. Apify has maintained, production-ready scrapers (actors)
  for Instagram public profiles, Facebook pages, TikTok profiles, and
  Google Maps that handle all anti-bot measures professionally.
  We don't need to build or maintain these — Apify handles it.

SECTION 5 — THE LANGGRAPH RESEARCH AGENT INTERNAL FLOW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

What is LangGraph?
  LangGraph is the framework we use to build the agent's logic as a
  "state machine" — a series of steps with a shared memory (state)
  that all steps can read from and write to. Think of it like a
  flowchart where each box is a step (node) and each arrow decides
  which step to go to next.

The Research Agent has 8 steps (nodes):

[START]
    │
    ▼
[NODE 1: EXTRACT_SEARCH_TERMS]
  What it does:
    Reads the form data (business name, industry, location, etc.)
    and uses Claude to generate smart, specific search queries
    for each of the 4 research lanes.

  Input:  form data from the database
  Output: a set of queries like this:
  {
    "business_queries": [
      "كافيه المحترم مصر الجديدة",
      "كافيه المحترم فيسبوك"
    ],
    "competitor_queries": [
      "كافيهات مصر الجديدة",
      "أفضل كافيه في مصر الجديدة"
    ],
    "market_queries": [
      "تسويق كافيهات مصر 2025",
      "instagram cafe egypt trends"
    ],
    "social_queries": [
      "#كافيهات_مصر_الجديدة",
      "#كافيه_مصر"
    ]
  }
    │
    ▼
[NODE 2-5: PARALLEL RESEARCH — all 4 lanes fire simultaneously]
  ├── Lane 1: research_digital_presence
  │     Uses: Tavily + Google Places + Apify
  │     Finds: business on Google Maps, Facebook, Instagram
  │
  ├── Lane 2: research_competitors
  │     Uses: Google Places Nearby Search + Tavily
  │     Finds: top 3-5 competitors in same area and category
  │
  ├── Lane 3: research_market_context
  │     Uses: Tavily
  │     Finds: industry trends, what's working in Egypt right now
  │
  └── Lane 4: research_social_signals
        Uses: Tavily + Apify
        Finds: trending content formats, popular hashtags

    │ (all 4 lanes finish, results merge)
    ▼
[NODE 6: AGGREGATE_AND_SCORE]
  What it does:
    Merges all the raw results from 4 lanes into one clean object.
    Scores the confidence level of each finding:
      - "high confidence": found on official source (Google, etc.)
      - "medium confidence": found on general web
      - "low confidence": inferred, not directly confirmed
    Flags low-confidence results as "don't reference directly"
    so the Discovery Agent doesn't state uncertain things as facts.
    │
    ▼
[NODE 7: GENERATE_INTELLIGENCE_REPORT]
  What it does:
    This is the most important step. Claude reads ALL the raw
    research and produces 3 critical outputs:

    1. SMART OBSERVATIONS
       Things the agent noticed that the client might not know.
       Written in Arabic, as if a consultant is briefing another.
       Examples:
         "لديه 94 تقييم على جوجل بمتوسط 4.3 — ده أساس كويس بس
          الـ listing ناقصة (مفيش منيو، مفيش صور كتير)"
         "فيسبوك موجود بـ 1,240 متابع بس آخر بوست من أسبوعين
          — الصفحة شبه ميتة"
         "مفيش إنستجرام خالص — وده أكبر gap في وجوده الرقمي"

    2. KNOWLEDGE GAPS
       Questions that the internet COULD NOT answer about this business.
       These become the Discovery Agent's priority questions to ask.
       Examples:
         "هل بيعمل ديلفري بنفسه أو مش مهتم بالموضوع ده؟"
         "ليه فيسبوك واقف من أسبوعين — مفيش وقت؟ مفيش أفكار؟"
         "هل يعرف إن المنافسين أقوى منه على إنستجرام؟"
         "ميزانيته اتفرقت على إيه بالظبط في الإعلانات اللي فشلت؟"
         "مين بيدير التواصل الرقمي — هو نفسه ولا في حد تاني؟"

    3. CONVERSATION HOOKS
       Specific opening lines the Discovery Agent can use that
       reference REAL information found in the research.
       Written as ready-to-use sentences for the agent.
       Examples:
         "لاحظت إن عندك صفحة فيسبوك بـ ١٢٠٠ متابع — ده شغل كويس.
          بس لاحظت كمان إن آخر بوست من أسبوعين — إيه اللي بيوقفك؟"
         "منافسيك في المنطقة معظمهم شغالين على إنستجرام — هل ده
          جه في بالك؟"
         "عندك ٩٤ تقييم على جوجل بـ ٤.٣ نجوم — ده رصيد حقيقي.
          إيه اللي الناس بتقوله فيك أكتر؟"
    │
    ▼
[NODE 8: STORE_TO_POSTGRESQL]
  What it does:
    Saves the complete Intelligence Report to the database.
    Notifies the frontend that research is done.
    The Discovery Agent will read this report before saying
    its first word in the chat.
    │
    ▼
[END → CHAT BECOMES AVAILABLE TO CLIENT]

into the final Business Profile JSON. This is what gets passed
to the Strategy Agent, Content Agent, and every other agent.

Three sources merged:
  1. Form data (what the client told us upfront)
  2. Research data (what we found online about them)
  3. Conversation data (what they revealed in the chat)

SECTION 11 — EDGE CASES & HOW TO HANDLE THEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EDGE CASE 1: Business not found online (new business or very offline)

  Research returns: all digital_presence fields = found: false

  Agent behavior:
    "شغلتك مش موجودة كتير على النت لسه — ده طبيعي في البداية.
     خليني أسألك: هل سبق وحاولت تبني أي وجود رقمي ولا لسه؟"

  The agent does NOT pretend to have found things it didn't find.
  It pivots gracefully to conversation-based discovery.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EDGE CASE 2: Wrong business found (common business name)

  The research might find a different "كافيه المحترم" in another city.
  Confidence scoring catches this (medium or low confidence).

  Agent uses a soft verification in the first message:
    "لاحظت كافيه اسمه كافيه المحترم في مصر الجديدة — هو ده؟"

  If user says no → agent discards research, proceeds without it.
  If user says yes → agent proceeds with research data.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EDGE CASE 3: Research takes too long (API timeout)

  Hard limit: 90 seconds maximum for the entire research process.

  If timeout occurs → store partial results → chat opens anyway.
  Agent opening message adapts:
    "تم إيجاد بعض المعلومات عن شغلتك..." (instead of full briefing)

  Chat still happens. Research data is partial but better than nothing.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EDGE CASE 4: Client uncomfortable with being researched

  Some clients might feel the AI is "spying" on them.

  Solution: Full transparency on the loading screen.
  Always show this text clearly:
    "بنبحث عن معلوماتك العامة المتاحة على الإنترنت فقط
     عشان نكون أكفأ في مساعدتك"

  Always provide the option: [ابدأ من غير بحث]
  If they opt out → agent proceeds with form data only.
