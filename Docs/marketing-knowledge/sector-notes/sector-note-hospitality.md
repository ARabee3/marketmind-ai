## When useful

Use this note for restaurants, cafés, bakeries, QSRs, and any business
selling food/drink prepared on premises — dine-in or delivery. It gives
sector-specific starting assumptions (Ramadan dependence, review velocity,
kitchen capacity as a marketing limit) that generic SME guidance misses.

## Poor-fit conditions

A poor fit for non-food hospitality (hotels, event venues — different
demand patterns) and for packaged-goods food businesses that are really
retail (use the retail note). Also a poor fit for a kitchen with no
capacity to add customers, because the guidance below assumes the kitchen
can absorb the plan's audience.

## Concrete sector-specific observations

1. **Visual platforms and Google Business Profile are the core stack.**
   Instagram/TikTok drive discovery, Google Business Profile captures
   intent-driven local search, and Facebook keeps the warm community. A
   hospitality SME ignoring GBP loses the cheapest intent channel.
2. **Reviews and reply speed are growth inputs.** answering reviews and
   message inquiries same-day materially improves conversion and local
   ranking; treating inbox as afterthought is the most common SME failure
   in this sector.
3. **Kitchen capacity is a marketing ceiling.** the volume a campaign can
   responsibly drive equals what the kitchen can produce at quality — a
   viral spike beyond capacity damages the brand, so campaigns should be
   paced to fulfillment, not just to budget.
4. **Delivery platforms and unit economics.** the platform commission must
   be checked against margin per order; a delivery channel growing gross
   volume at negative per-order margin is not progress (see
   `channel-delivery-platforms`). Capture a direct contact from delivery
   customers or the platform owns the relationship.
5. **Ramadan and Eid shape the whole year.** for F&B, Ramadan is often the
   single largest revenue window (iftar/suhoor, family gatherings); the
   12-week plan must either lean into it or choose a window that avoids a
   direct comparison with it. Summer and winter holidays are secondary.

## Explicit limitation

This is a starting point, not a complete hospitality strategy. Combine
with situation diagnosis, STP, the chosen objective, and the relevant
channel playbooks before producing a plan.
