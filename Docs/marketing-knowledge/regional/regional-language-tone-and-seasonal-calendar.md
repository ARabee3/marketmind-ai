## When useful

Use this entry whenever a plan involves language/tone choice or any
seasonal timing in Egypt — when to push, when to soften, when to launch,
when to schedule content around holidays. Language here means Egyptian
Arabic dialect (masry), not Modern Standard Arabic, for any SME speaking to
Egyptian consumers.

## Poor-fit conditions

Do **not** apply Ramadan or seasonal cadence guidance to a B2B or
professional-services business whose customers have no real seasonal pattern
— for those, forcing a Ramadan angle is decorative, not strategic. Do not
use formal MSA (fusha) as a default for an Egyptian SME consumer audience;
it reads as distant/official rather than local/familiar. And do not assume
every seasonal window is profitable for every business — Ramadan is huge
for food and hospitality and slow for some professional services; the
guidance must match the segment.

## Specific, concrete guidance

**Language and tone.** Default to Egyptian Arabic dialect for consumer
SMEs; it is the register customers speak and trust. Keep tone direct,
warm, and specific, not slogans — concrete product language ("fresh
today", "deliver to your door") over abstract claims ("the best
experience"). For mixed-language posts, common practice is Arabic body with
selective English/numerals — acceptable, but do not switch mid-sentence
arbitrarily. For healthcare, education, and formal professional services,
tone can be slightly more formal but should stay Egyptian and
conversational, not stiff. Never invent a numeric claim to sound
authoritative.

**Seasonal calendar (concrete windows).**

- **Ramadan** — pacing, not silence: the first week is preparation and
  announcements, mid-Ramadan is the conversion window for food/qsr/bakery
  and gifts, the last 10 days and Eid prep is the peak for retail. For
  non-food businesses, soften cadence (people's attention shifts); do not
  run aggressive acquisition as if it were a normal month. Iftar and suhoor
  timing matters for food businesses.
- **Eid al-Fitr** — short burst window immediately after Ramadan; retail
  and gifting peak before and during the first day, then taper.
- **Eid al-Adha** — different buyer (meat, livestock, gifts, clothing);
  relevant to retail and hospitality; not relevant to most services.
- **Back to school** — late August to mid-September; education,
  uniforms/stationery retail, tutoring centers; not a window for food
  businesses.
- **Summer** — seasonality for hospitality, tourism-adjacent, gyms (low),
  AC sales (high); plan around, not against.
- **Winter holidays** — narrow in Egypt but present for gifts/hospitality;
  avoid assuming Christmas-level consumer behavior; keep proportionate.

For each applicable season an entry should set an effective/expiry window or
note it in the plan's 12-week calendar. For evergreen businesses simply set
`seasons: []` and do not force a seasonal angle.
