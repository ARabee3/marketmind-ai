## When useful

Use this entry whenever a plan involves language/tone choice or seasonal
timing in Egypt — when to push, when to soften, when to launch, and when to
schedule content around holidays. Choose dialect, Modern Standard Arabic, or
mixed language from the audience, sector, and trust requirement; do not treat
one register as universal for every Egyptian SME.

## Poor-fit conditions

Do **not** apply Ramadan or seasonal cadence guidance to a B2B or
professional-services business whose customers have no real seasonal pattern
— for those, forcing a Ramadan angle is decorative, not strategic. Do not
assume every seasonal window is profitable for every business; the guidance
must match the segment, capacity, and offer.

## Specific, concrete guidance

**Language and tone.** Start with the customer's spoken register and the
business's trust requirement. Egyptian colloquial can support familiar
consumer copy; Modern Standard Arabic can fit formal, regulated, or official
contexts; mixed Arabic/English can fit categories where customers already use
that vocabulary. Keep tone direct, warm, and specific, not slogans — concrete
product language ("fresh today", "deliver to your door") over abstract claims
("the best experience"). Never invent a numeric claim to sound authoritative.

**Evergreen selection rules.**

- Use a seasonal angle only when the business has a real seasonal offer,
  buyer need, capacity plan, or calendar constraint.
- For evergreen businesses, set `seasons: []` and keep the plan focused on
  the objective rather than adding decorative Ramadan, Eid, or school themes.
- For seasonal businesses, use the sources below as context, then confirm
  the exact dates, demand pattern, stock, staffing, and fulfillment capacity
  for the current planning cycle.

**2026/2027 planning-cycle windows.**

- **Ramadan** — pacing, not silence: the first week can be preparation and
  announcements, mid-Ramadan can be a conversion window for relevant
  food/qsr/bakery and gift offers, while the last 10 days and Eid prep can
  matter for relevant retail categories. NTRA and Ipsos both show
  Ramadan-specific digital and shopping behaviour, but that context is not a
  guarantee of demand for every SME. For non-food businesses, test whether
  attention and offer fit support a seasonal push before increasing cadence.
- **Eid al-Fitr** — short burst window immediately after Ramadan; retail
  and gifting may peak before and during the first day, then taper.
- **Eid al-Adha** — different buyer mix (meat, livestock, gifts, clothing);
  relevant to some retail and hospitality offers, not to most services.
- **Back to school** — verify the Ministry of Education calendar each cycle;
  the cited 2025/2026 example scheduled the new academic year for September
  20, 2025. Education, uniforms/stationery retail, and tutoring centers may
  plan before the official start; food businesses should not force this angle.
- **Summer** — relevant only where heat, travel, tourism, sports, or category
  usage changes the buyer need; plan around real capacity and demand signals.
- **Winter holidays** — narrow in Egypt but present for gifts/hospitality;
  avoid assuming Christmas-level consumer behavior; keep proportionate.

For each applicable season an entry should set an effective/expiry window or
note it in the plan's 12-week calendar. For evergreen businesses simply set
`seasons: []`.
