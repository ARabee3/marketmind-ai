## When useful

Use this playbook whenever a plan needs a KPI — which is every plan. It is
what stops an owner from reporting "likes grew" while orders stayed flat.
Always pair an objective with one business KPI, and where useful one
diagnostic KPI that explains movement in the business KPI.

## Poor-fit conditions

A poor fit when there is no business action that can be counted — without a
countable business action (an order, a booking, a first-time customer, a
return visit, a review), measurement defaults to vanity and the plan
becomes unreviewable. Fix the counting first, then choose a KPI. Also a poor
fit when the owner only wants to track follower growth; follower count is
not a business outcome.

## Required inputs

- The chosen objective and its funnel stage.
- The countable business action that defines success (e.g. new
  customers/week, conversion rate, repeat rate).
- A baseline of that action measured in the first two weeks.
- A target mode: `establish_baseline` (just measure honestly for the
  window) or `owner_target` (a number the owner commits to reach), rather
  than `verified_benchmark_range` for any metric without a verified Egypt
  benchmark.

## Worked micro-example (illustrative — not real EGP figures)

> The numbers below are illustrative placeholders chosen to show the
> shape, not verified benchmarks or real results.

Suppose a café chooses the conversion objective. Baseline: in weeks 1–2 the
owner counts **40 walk-in orders/week** and **25 message starts/week** of
which **10** become orders. The plan's business KPI is **orders from new
message-starts/week** (target mode `owner_target` = 15 by week 12). The
diagnostic KPI is **reply speed** (are messages answered within same-day),
because slow replies are the suspected blocker. The plan reports the
business KPI each week against baseline; if it doesn't move, the diagnostic
tells the owner where to look. There is **no** engagement-rate or CPC
benchmark applied here — those would be unverifiable vanity comparisons
(see `engagement-rate-benchmark-caveat.md`).
