## Fit signals (when this objective is right now)

Conversion is the right priority when the business already gets attention
and interested visitors but they do not turn into paying customers —
messages that go unanswered, website/landing visits that bounce, footfall
that does not buy, calls that do not become bookings. The bottleneck is the
turn, not the reach. Concrete signals: profile views or messages are
healthy but completed orders are flat; a delivery menu that is hard to
read; a website with traffic but no working call-to-action; offers that are
noticed but never redeemed.

## Poor-fit signals

Conversion is a poor fit when there is no traffic or interest to convert —
that is an awareness or acquisition problem first. It is also a poor fit
when the conversion problem is operational, not marketing: the food is
late, the phone is busy, the stock is out. Fixing operations is not a
marketing objective and a conversion plan will not save it. Conversion is
also a poor fit when there is no historical baseline of how many visitors
vs. customers currently convert, because without a baseline the
improvement cannot be measured.

## Typical funnel stage(s)

Conversion owns the bottom of the funnel: the rate at which interested
people complete a paid action, measured as conversion rate (visitors →
customers) or as order completion rate (started → completed order).

## What a 12-week plan under this objective usually prioritizes

A 12-week conversion plan usually prioritizes: (1) measuring the current
drop in the funnel — where do interested people stop (no answer, no order,
no show); (2) fixing the cheapest conversion blockers first — a clear
price list, a working WhatsApp, fast replies, a simple order path, a clear
address; (3) testing a single conversion-focused change at a time with a
defined before/after window, not ten changes at once; (4) a conversion
offer that reduces friction (clear hours, easy payment, a reason to act
now). The KPI is conversion rate or completed-order rate, measured against
the baseline taken in weeks 1–2.

## Risks of choosing this objective when it doesn't fit

Choosing conversion when reach is the bottleneck optimizes a funnel that
isn't being fed. Choosing conversion while running many untested changes
simultaneously makes it impossible to attribute any improvement. Choosing
conversion when the real problem is operational quality (speed, stock,
service) produces a plan that improved the rate of disappointing customers,
which is not progress.
