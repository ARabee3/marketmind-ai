## Use cases (fit)

Google Business Profile (GBP) is the right channel for any local physical
business that benefits from being found by people who are actively
searching near them — restaurants, cafés, salons, clinics, home services,
retail with a storefront. It is one of the highest-value organic channels
per hour invested because it captures intent: people searching "restaurant
near me" or a business by name are already in the consideration stage.
It is also where customer reviews accrue, which influences conversion and
SEO.

## Poor-fit conditions

GBP is a poor fit for a pure-online business with no physical address or
service area, or for a business whose customers never search by category or
location (rare). It is also effectively absent when the owner claims the
profile but never completes it — an empty GBP with no photos and no hours
is worse than none, because it converts intent to a loss.

## Required assets

A verified, complete profile: accurate name, category, address or service
area, hours (kept updated, especially holidays and Ramadan), phone,
website link, a real set of photos of the space and products, and the
ability to ask satisfied customers for a review. Posts and offers can be
added but are secondary to the completeness and accuracy of the core info.

## Team effort / capacity implications

GBP is one of the lowest ongoing-capacity channels for the value it
returns: a few hours to set it up well, then minutes per week or month to
keep hours and photos fresh, plus the habit of asking happy customers for a
review. This is the channel an SME with no team should still maintain.

## Organic vs. paid role

GBP itself is organic — appearing in local results and Maps is free and
earned through accuracy, reviews, and proximity. There is a paid layer
(Search/Maps ads) but for most Egyptian SME budgets the organic profile is
the high-return baseline; paid is optional and secondary.

## Budget notes

GBP fits the `organic_only` budget mode — there is no required spend to
gain most of its value. Optional paid local ads are only worth considering
once the organic profile is complete and reviewed.

## Measurement

Measure directional queries (how many people found the business via
search vs. direct), profile views, calls, and direction requests. The most
useful business KPI is "calls and direction requests per week" because
those are intent-ready actions; tie that to actual visits or orders when
possible. No verified Egypt reach benchmark exists for GBP, so measure
against the business's own baseline.

## Risks

The main risks are letting the profile go stale (wrong hours, especially
during Ramadan or holidays, actively lose customers), ignoring reviews
(positive ones unacknowledged, negative ones unanswered), and treating GBP
as set-and-forget. A dormant GBP quietly loses ranking to competitors who
keep theirs updated.
