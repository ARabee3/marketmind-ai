## Use cases (fit)

Google Business Profile (GBP) is a strong fit for eligible local physical or
service-area businesses that benefit from being found by people who are actively
searching near them — restaurants, cafés, salons, clinics, home services,
retail with a storefront. It is a practical organic baseline because it
captures intent: people searching "restaurant near me" or a business by
name are already in the consideration stage. It is also where customer
reviews accrue; review count and ratings can support local prominence and
influence conversion, but they are not a standalone SEO guarantee.

## Poor-fit conditions

GBP is a poor fit for a pure-online business with no physical address or
service area, or for a business whose customers never search by category or
location. It is also weak when the owner claims the
profile but never completes it — an empty GBP with no photos and no hours
can convert intent into a loss.

## Required assets

A verified, complete profile: accurate name, category, address or service
area, hours (kept updated, especially holidays and Ramadan), phone,
website link, a real set of photos of the space and products, and the
ability to ask satisfied customers for a review. Posts and offers can be
added but are secondary to the completeness and accuracy of the core info.

## Team effort / capacity implications

GBP is a low ongoing-capacity local channel: a few hours to set it up well,
then minutes per week or month to keep hours and photos fresh, plus the habit
of asking happy customers for a review. It is a practical baseline for an
eligible local SME with no team.

## Organic vs. paid role

GBP itself is organic — appearing in local results and Maps is free for
eligible storefront or service-area businesses. Local visibility depends on
relevance, distance, and prominence; reviews and positive ratings can help
prominence, while paid Search/Maps ads are optional and secondary.

## Budget notes

GBP fits the `organic_only` budget mode — there is no required spend to gain
value from an accurate profile. Optional paid local ads are only worth
considering once the organic profile is complete and reviewed.

## Measurement

Measure Search and Maps views, searches, directions, calls, website clicks,
messages, and bookings where available. The most useful business KPI is the
count of intent-ready actions per week, such as calls, directions,
messages, website clicks, or bookings; tie that to actual visits or orders
when possible. No verified Egypt reach benchmark exists for GBP, so measure
against the business's own baseline.

## Risks

The main risks are letting the profile go stale (wrong hours, especially
during Ramadan or holidays, actively lose customers), ignoring reviews
(positive ones unacknowledged, negative ones unanswered), and treating GBP
as set-and-forget. A dormant GBP quietly loses trust and visibility against
competitors who keep theirs accurate.
