## Use cases (fit)

TikTok is a useful awareness channel when the target segment skews young
and the business can show itself in motion — short, real, behind-the-scenes
or satisfying-process video (food being made, a salon transformation, a
gym session, a tutor explaining one thing). It fits visual, energetic,
younger-skewing businesses best, and can drive disproportionate discovery
because the algorithm pushes content beyond followers.

## Poor-fit conditions

TikTok is a poor fit when the owner is uncomfortable on camera and has no
helper who can film, because the channel rewards authentic motion, not
static graphics. It is a poor fit for a target segment that is not on the
platform (older, B2B, formal professional services). It is also a poor fit
when the business cannot absorb a sudden awareness spike — a viral TikTok
that brings footfall to a one-cook kitchen harms the business, so capacity
to serve must be real before the channel is opened.

## Required assets

A complete profile, a willingness to film real moments, and a clear
offramp (a way to convert attention into a business action: WhatsApp, an
address, a clear working order path). No production studio is needed; a
phone and a person willing to be in the frame is the minimum.

## Team effort / capacity implications

TikTok is capacity-intensive because it rewards cadence (several videos
per week) and responsiveness. It is rarely a solo-owner channel unless the
owner genuinely enjoys making the content. For most SMEs it should be a
deliberate add-on with clear weekly time set aside, not a half-managed
extra.

## Organic vs. paid role

TikTok organic can produce large reach without paid spend — that is its
primary draw for SMEs without budget. Paid TikTok ads extend control over
targeting and reach but require a real budget and creative; for small
budgets the organic upside is usually the better first bet.

## Budget notes

Organic-only is a legitimate budget mode on TikTok. For paid, no verified
Egypt-specific reach, CPC, or CPM benchmark is available (see
`engagement-rate-benchmark-caveat.md`), so budget planning again starts from
a small test and a measured cost per outcome, not from an assumed rate.

## Measurement

For awareness: views, profile visits, follows. For acquisition: clicks to
the offramp and the counted business action those turn into. Because
TikTok's audience is young and the conversion path is indirect, the metric
to watch is the offramp click and the resulting business action, not views
in isolation. A viral video with no offramp produces no customers.

## Risks

The principal risks are capacity mismatch (a spike that the business
cannot serve), investing creator time without a conversion offramp (views
that go nowhere), and chasing virality with content that misrepresents the
business and sets expectations the experience cannot meet.
