## Use cases (fit)

An owned simple website or landing page is useful when the business needs a
convertible home off-platform: a place to send people from social ads and
organic posts where the call-to-action is clear and uninterrupted. It fits
businesses with a product catalogue (ecommerce), a service that needs
explanation (professional services, clinics, tutoring, home services), or
any business whose conversion path should not rely on a third-party app's
inbox. It is also the destination every other channel's offramp should point
to.

## Poor-fit conditions

A website is a poor fit when the owner has no one to maintain it and it
will go stale, or when the business converts entirely inside another app
(a one-product café that takes orders only via WhatsApp and has no catalog
does not need a curated site — a strong GBP and a pinned WhatsApp link will
do). It is also a poor fit when the budget cannot sustain hosting and a
basics update, producing a broken site that reads as abandoned (worse than
no site).

## Required assets

A clear domain or at least a stable link, a fast mobile-first page, one
clear call-to-action (call, message, order, visit), accurate hours,
address, a short product or service description, real photos, and a
working contact path. The minimum viable site is often one page, not
multi-page.

## Team effort / capacity implications

A simple one-page site is low ongoing capacity once built (a few checks per
year for hours and links), but it requires upfront effort or help to build.
A multi-page or ecommerce site is higher capacity and needs someone
responsible for content and stock updates, comparable to a half-channel.

## Organic vs. paid role

A website can earn organic traffic via SEO and local search (especially with
a linked GBP), but for most SMEs it is primarily a destination that other
channels drive traffic to. Paid traffic without a working site is wasted
budget; fix the site before paying to send people to it.

## Budget notes

`organic_only` covers a low-cost hosted one-pager. `monthly_amount` may
apply if paid traffic is being sent to it. `scenario_only` is appropriate
for a launch or seasonal landing page that runs only for a window. No
verified Egypt CPC/CPM benchmark exists (see `engagement-rate-benchmark-caveat.md`),
so budget from a small test and a measured cost per outcome.

## Measurement

Measure unique visitors, the conversion event (calls/ orders/ bookings per
week) and the conversion rate of visitors → that action. The most useful
KPI is the conversion rate of the destination page, because improving the
page is cheaper than buying more traffic to a broken one. Measure bounce on
the action page specifically, not site-wide.

## Risks

The main risks are building a site then never updating it (becomes a
credibility liability), driving paid traffic to a page whose call-to-action
or phone is broken, and over-building (a five-page site that says the same
thing five ways) which raises maintenance cost without improving
conversion.
