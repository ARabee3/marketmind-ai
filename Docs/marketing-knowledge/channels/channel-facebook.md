## Use cases (fit)

Facebook is a useful lead channel when the target segment is broad (not a
teen-only audience) and the business depends on local discovery or
community. It fits local retail, food and beverage, salons, home services,
and any business whose catchment is a neighbourhood or city. It is also a
good supporting channel for keeping an existing community warm (page
updates, hours, photos, replies) and a good acquisition channel through
geotargeted reach to a defined area. Facebook's audience in Egypt is large
and skews adult — see `benchmark-egypt-facebook-reach.md` for the verified
reach figure.

## Poor-fit conditions

Facebook is a poor fit when the target segment is overwhelmingly teenagers,
because that audience is not primarily on Facebook. It is a poor fit when
the owner has no time to manage a page at all and no budget to run any
paid effort — a dormant page that never updates reads as "closed or
abandoned" and harms trust. It is also a poor fit when the conversion path
goes through a different owned channel (e.g. a strong website with search
traffic) and Facebook only adds noise.

## Required assets

A complete Facebook Page: recognizable name and profile/cover photo, an
accurate short description, address, hours, a working WhatsApp/phone link,
a few clear product or service photos, and at minimum a pinned post
explaining what the business is. For paid efforts, a Business Manager and a
payment method in EGP are required.

## Team effort / capacity implications

A page can be sustained by a solo owner with one to two short posts per
week plus prompt replies to messages — the real ongoing cost is reply
speed, not volume. Paid campaigns need a few hours set up front and a
weekly check on budget burn and results, not daily attention. Facebook is a
medium-low capacity channel for an SME.

## Organic vs. paid role

Organic Facebook reach for a business page is low without paid support; a
page that posts organically only reaches a fraction of its existing
followers and almost no new people. Organic is most valuable for keeping a
warm community, posting hours and updates, and reply responsiveness. Paid
is what moves reach and acquisition at a city scale for a real budget; pure
organic will not produce measurable acquisition within a 12-week window for
most businesses.

## Budget notes

For an organic-only owner there is no cash cost but the reach ceiling is
real. For a paid effort, size the monthly budget to what the owner can
sustain without interrupting it mid-plan (a stopped-and-started budget
produces erratic results). There is no verified Egypt-specific CPC/CPM
benchmark available (see `engagement-rate-benchmark-caveat.md`), so budget
planning should start from a small test budget and a measured
cost-per-new-customer outcome, not from an assumed rate.

## Measurement

Measure reach and profile views for awareness; message starts and clicks
for acquisition; and, for paid, cost per new customer as the real business
KPI. Facebook metrics do not equal business outcomes — starting a chat is
not a sale. Always tie a Facebook metric back to a counted business action
(an order, a booking) rather than reporting engagement alone.

## Risks

The main risk is treating Facebook engagement (likes, reactions) as a
business result; it is not. A second risk is over-investing in paid before
the conversion path works (busy inbox, clear hours). A third is letting the
page go dormant, which reads as "closed" to a new visitor.
