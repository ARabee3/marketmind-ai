## When useful

Use this playbook once the objective, audience, and channel(s) are chosen —
content pillars are the connective tissue between positioning and the
actual posts. It is useful when an owner says "I don't know what to post"
but does know their business; pillars turn that known material into a
repeatable plan instead of a daily improvisation.

## Poor-fit conditions

This is a poor fit before an objective and audience are chosen — content
pillars without a target segment and a single objective produce generic
content. It is also a poor fit for a single big campaign (launch) where one
message repeated over a window is the plan; pillars are for ongoing
cadence, not a one-window campaign.

## Capacity implications

Strategy still defines three to five content pillars. A key capacity variable
is how many of those pillars can be active in a given week. Use these
illustrative cadence tiers as starting points based on real weekly hours, not
aspiration:

- **Solo owner, ~1 hour/week** — for example, one post per week plus daily
  replies; rotate one active pillar at a time (e.g. "what we sell and how to
  order").
- **Solo owner + part-time helper, ~3–5 hours/week** — for example,
  two-to-three posts plus one reel per week; keep a two-pillar active weekly
  subset (e.g. the product + the people/behind the scenes).
- **Dedicated one-person social role, ~10+ hours/week** — for example,
  four-to-five posts plus two reels/stories per week; cover the full three to
  five content pillars across the month.

Capacity changes weekly output and cadence, not the Strategy output contract.
A pillar each for the product (what it is, how to order), the proof (reviews,
photos, results), and the person (the owner, the space, behind the scenes)
covers many SMEs, while higher-capacity teams may add extra pillars within the
three-to-five-pillar strategy. Low capacity should rotate the active weekly
subset rather than delete pillars from the plan.

## How it hands off to the Content Agent

This playbook stops at planning. It defines pillars, cadence tier, and
which channels — it does not generate actual captions, scripts, or posts.
The Content Agent downstream receives: the chosen pillars, the cadence
target, the channel(s), the audience, the positioning line from STP, and
the constraints (hours available, language, Ramadan/holiday timing from
`regional-language-tone-and-seasonal-calendar`). It is then the Content
Agent's job to draft within that frame, and the owner's job to approve
before anything is published. Nothing in this entry should be read as
"generate the posts now."
