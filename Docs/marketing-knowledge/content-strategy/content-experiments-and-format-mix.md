## When useful

Use this playbook once pillars and cadence are set, to decide which formats
(reels, single images, stories, text, link posts) to try and how to learn
cheaply which one is worth doubling down on. It is useful when an owner
wants variety but has limited capacity, because the alternative — posting
random formats — burns capacity without learning.

## Poor-fit conditions

A poor fit when capacity is so low (solo, ~1 hour/week) that there is no
room to test more than one format — for that tier, single-format cadence
beats experiments. Also a poor fit before pillars exist: experiments
without pillars test random variables. And a poor fit when the business
cannot measure any outcome of a format (no profile views, no clicks, no
business action) — an experiment without a measurable outcome is
unresolvable.

## Capacity implications

Run one experiment at a time, with two or three variants, over at least two
weeks. More variants than that cannot be read at SME volume — the signal
drowns in noise. Keep the chosen metrics minimal: one awareness proxy
(reach/views/profile visits) and one conversion proxy (click/DM/business
action), measured per format. Do not run multi-format experiments while
also changing offers, captions, or audience targeting — isolate one
variable.

## How it hands off to the Content Agent

This playbook defines which format experiments to run, for how long, and
what metric decides the winner — it stops there. The Content Agent drafts
the variant posts/reels for the experiment within the pillar frame; it does
not run the experiment itself or interpret the result. The owner (or the
Strategy step) reviews the metric after the window, picks the winner, and
the Content Agent then defaults to the winning format. No generated post or
reel becomes the experiment's "result"; the result is the measured
business metric, read by the owner.
