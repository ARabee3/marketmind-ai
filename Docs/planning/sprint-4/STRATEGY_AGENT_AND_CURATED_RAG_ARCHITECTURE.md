# Strategy Agent and Curated RAG Architecture

**Status:** Sprint 4 implementation source of truth

**Audience:** Product, marketing, frontend, backend, AI, and reviewers

**Replaces:** Earlier Strategy notes that deferred Qdrant beyond the Strategy slice

**Quick team guide:** `STRATEGY_AGENT_TEAM_GUIDE.md`

**GitHub execution map:**
[#66 — Strategy Agent + curated RAG vertical slice](https://github.com/ARabee3/marketmind-ai/issues/66)

## 1. Decision summary

MarketMind will implement a small, real, evaluated RAG system as part of the
first Strategy Agent vertical slice.

The architecture separates four things that must not be confused:

| Concept | Meaning | Where it lives |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Business Profile | Exact, owner-confirmed facts and evidence from Discovery | PostgreSQL; passed directly to Strategy |
| Rules | Non-negotiable product constraints | NestJS/FastAPI code and validators |
| Playbooks | Reviewed, reusable marketing guidance | PostgreSQL source records plus a derived Qdrant index |
| Strategy | A custom 12-week plan for one business | Versioned Strategy records in PostgreSQL |

The confirmed Business Profile is **not** embedded into Qdrant in v1. It is a
bounded, structured, immutable input and every important field must be supplied
directly. Profile fields are used to build a privacy-minimized retrieval query
that searches the larger marketing knowledge collection.

Qdrant is a derived search index, not a source of truth. PostgreSQL owns
knowledge approval, versions, source references, expiry, strategy versions, and
owner decisions.

## 2. Plain-language explanation

### 2.1 What is a playbook?

A playbook is a reusable marketing recipe. It explains when a method is useful,
when it is a poor fit, what inputs it needs, and how to measure it.

Examples:

- how to select a primary audience with STP;
- when Instagram is useful for a local business;
- how a small team can choose a sustainable content cadence;
- how to establish a KPI baseline without inventing a benchmark;
- how to create conservative, base, and growth budget scenarios.

A playbook is not a complete plan for Koshary Corner or any other business.

### 2.2 What is a strategy?

A strategy is the one custom plan generated by combining:

1. the complete confirmed Business Profile;
2. the owner's Strategy Brief;
3. optional evidence from a future Research Pack;
4. relevant playbooks retrieved through RAG;
5. deterministic scoring and validation rules.

The Strategy Agent produces a decision for this business now. The playbook
remains reusable for another business later.

### 2.3 What is RAG doing?

RAG searches the reviewed MarketMind marketing handbook before the model writes
the strategy.

```text
Confirmed Business Profile from PostgreSQL
              |
              +--> exact context supplied to Strategy
              |
              +--> small retrieval query derived from relevant fields
                              |
                              v
Reviewed playbooks --> PostgreSQL --> chunk/embed --> Qdrant
                              |                       |
                              +---- retrieve ---------+
                                      |
                                      v
                             cited knowledge pack
                                      |
                                      v
                           Strategy generation + rules
```

RAG does not approve knowledge, decide strategy, or execute marketing. It only
finds relevant reviewed knowledge.

## 3. Strategy responsibility and boundaries

### Goal

Turn the confirmed Discovery profile, owner choices, and retrieved marketing
knowledge into one explainable 12-week marketing plan.

### Required inputs

- immutable `BusinessProfileVersion`;
- `StrategyBrief`;
- `RetrievedKnowledgePack` from curated RAG;
- optional `ResearchPack` when the separate Research Agent exists.

### Output

One versioned `StrategyPlan` containing:

- executive summary;
- situation diagnosis;
- one primary objective and funnel stage;
- target audience and positioning;
- maximum two primary channels and one supporting channel;
- tone, language, and three to five content pillars;
- 12-week themes, formats, cadence, and bounded experiments;
- organic-only or external-spend budget scenarios;
- KPI and measurement plan;
- assumptions, risks, blockers, and citations.

### Forbidden behavior

- changing confirmed Business Profile facts;
- inventing missing owner facts, benchmarks, or sources;
- performing fresh competitor research inside Strategy;
- generating final captions, posts, scripts, or assets;
- executing ads or moving money;
- publishing content;
- advancing without explicit owner approval.

## 4. Actual Discovery profile input

Strategy receives the complete real profile defined by the Discovery contracts:

- identity;
- offer, best sellers, price range, and purchase occasions;
- customer segments, needs, peak periods, and order occasions;
- differentiation, customer choice reasons, and proof points;
- active channels, current activities, delivery platforms, and available assets;
- growth goals, timeframe, budget range, team capacity, and constraints;
- evidence-aware competitor, local-demand, and digital-presence signals;
- accepted research observations;
- unresolved uncertainties;
- owner goals and strategy-relevant notes.

These fields are provided directly from PostgreSQL. Retrieval is never trusted
to recover a budget, constraint, uncertainty, or confirmed fact.

Only a privacy-minimized summary is embedded as the RAG query. It may contain
business type, market, locale, objective, funnel stage, active channels, asset
capability, team capacity, and budget mode. It must exclude owner personal data,
exact address text, credentials, and unrelated private notes.

## 5. Strategy Brief

The Strategy Brief is separate from the confirmed profile so a new planning
choice does not rewrite Discovery truth.

Required fields:

| Field | Values or behavior |
| --- | --- |
| `business_profile_version_id` | Immutable confirmed profile version |
| `primary_objective` | One owner-selected objective |
| `start_date` | Beginning of the 12-week period |
| `plan_language` | `ar-EG`, `en`, or `mixed` |
| `paid_media_allowed` | Explicit boolean |
| `external_budget_mode` | `organic_only`, `monthly_amount`, `three_month_amount`, or `scenario_only` |
| `external_budget_egp` | Optional owner amount or range |
| `team_capacity` | Owner-confirmed production/response capacity |
| `constraints` | Additional operational or compliance limits |
| `clarification_answers` | Answers to Strategy-only questions |

Critical unresolved choices can allow draft generation for discussion, but they
must remain visible blockers and prevent approval.

## 6. Marketing method used by Strategy

The methodology is adaptive. It uses the smallest useful framework for the
decision instead of producing a generic consultant report.

1. **Situation:** concise 5Cs plus opportunity/risk synthesis.
2. **Audience:** segmentation, targeting, and customer needs.
3. **Positioning:** value proposition plus only relevant 7Ps checks.
4. **Objective:** one SMART-style goal mapped to a funnel stage.
5. **Channels:** deterministic scorecard based on fit and constraints.
6. **Messaging:** tone, language, value proposition, and content pillars.
7. **Execution:** weekly themes, formats, cadence, and experiments.
8. **Budget:** organic-only or conservative/base/growth external-spend scenarios.
9. **Measurement:** funnel KPIs with evidence-tiered targets.

PESTEL, detailed 7Ps, and specialist frameworks are retrieved only when the
business situation makes them relevant and the knowledge is reviewed.

## 7. What goes into the curated RAG library

The first corpus is a reviewed MarketMind marketing handbook. Coverage matters
more than document volume.

### Foundation playbooks

- 5Cs and concise SWOT synthesis;
- STP and customer-needs analysis;
- positioning and value proposition;
- relevant 7Ps checks;
- SMART objectives and funnel mapping.

### Objective playbooks

- awareness;
- acquisition;
- conversion/sales;
- retention/repeat behavior;
- offer or business launch.

### Channel playbooks

- Facebook;
- Instagram;
- TikTok;
- Google Business Profile/Maps;
- website or landing page;
- delivery platforms where relevant.

Each channel playbook explains use cases, poor-fit conditions, objectives,
required assets, team effort, organic/paid roles, measurement, and risks.

### Content-strategy playbooks

- selecting three to five content pillars;
- format mix;
- sustainable cadence by team capacity;
- weekly themes;
- controlled experiment design;
- handoff boundary to the Content Agent.

### Budget and measurement playbooks

- organic-only planning;
- conservative/base/growth scenario construction;
- channel allocation arithmetic;
- baseline establishment;
- owner targets;
- baseline improvement;
- verified benchmark ranges;
- vanity metric versus business metric guidance.

### Egypt/MENA and sector playbooks

- Arabic, English, and mixed-language guidance;
- local tone and cultural considerations;
- reviewed seasonal guidance with effective/expiry dates;
- initial sector notes for retail, hospitality, services, education, and
  healthcare.

### Benchmarks and policies

- numeric benchmarks only from current reviewed sources;
- source/citation policy;
- owner-approval policy;
- platform or compliance guidance with expiry dates.

Critical rules such as approval, paid-media prohibition, the 2+1 channel limit,
and citation requirements are also enforced in code. Retrieving a policy is not
the safety boundary.

### Explicit exclusions for v1

Do not ingest:

- generated Strategy plans;
- unreviewed AI output;
- random marketing blogs;
- private profiles from other businesses;
- copied competitor strategies;
- outdated platform claims;
- unsupported market statistics;
- generated captions or content calendars.

Anonymized, owner-approved historical plans may become clearly labeled case
studies later, after leakage and quality policies exist.

## 8. Knowledge governance in PostgreSQL

`MarketingKnowledgeEntry` is the authoritative reviewed record.

Minimum fields:

- `id`, `slug`, `version`, `kind`, `title`, `summary`, and `body`;
- locale, market, industry, objective, funnel, channel, season, and budget tags;
- evidence tier and source references;
- `draft`, `approved`, `retired`, or `expired` review status;
- effective and expiry timestamps;
- author, reviewer, review timestamp, and checksum.

`MarketingKnowledgeChunk` stores the deterministic chunk projection:

- chunk ID, entry/version ID, order, text, token count, checksum;
- embedding provider/model/dimensions;
- Qdrant point ID and indexing timestamp.

`KnowledgeIngestionRun` records ingestion status, counts, errors, configuration,
and the actor or commit that initiated it.

Only `approved`, effective, non-expired entry versions may be indexed for live
Strategy retrieval.

## 9. Ingestion pipeline

The initial reviewed source pack lives under `Docs/marketing-knowledge/` as
Markdown with validated front matter. A team member must understand and review
each document before it becomes approved knowledge.

The idempotent ingestion command performs:

1. validate required metadata and source references;
2. normalize whitespace and preserve Arabic/English sections;
3. split by semantic headings and paragraphs;
4. create chunks of approximately 300-500 tokens with small boundary overlap;
5. avoid splitting a rule, table row group, or citation from its explanation;
6. generate multilingual embeddings;
7. upsert PostgreSQL knowledge versions and chunks transactionally;
8. upsert matching Qdrant points using stable UUIDs;
9. mark removed or superseded versions unavailable;
10. write an ingestion report and fail visibly on partial indexing.

The default production embedding configuration is
`text-embedding-3-large`, chosen for English and non-English retrieval. Tests
use a deterministic fake embedding provider. The provider and dimensions are
configuration values; changing either creates a new collection version and a
full re-index rather than mixing incompatible vectors.

## 10. Qdrant derived index

Initial collection: `marketing_knowledge_v1`.

Each Qdrant point contains one chunk vector plus a payload copy:

- `chunk_id`, `entry_id`, `entry_version`, and `checksum`;
- `text` for retrieval assembly;
- `kind`, `locale`, `markets`, `industries`, and `business_models`;
- `objectives`, `funnel_stages`, `channels`, `seasons`, and `budget_modes`;
- `evidence_tier`, `review_status`, `effective_at`, and `expires_at`.

Create payload indexes before ingestion for frequently filtered fields,
especially status, dates, kind, market, locale, industry, objective, funnel,
channel, and evidence tier.

PostgreSQL remains authoritative. Qdrant can be deleted and rebuilt without
losing approved knowledge.

## 11. Retrieval behavior

### Query construction

FastAPI builds a retrieval request from the Strategy Brief and relevant profile
fields. It creates focused subqueries for:

- diagnosis/framework;
- objective and funnel;
- each candidate channel;
- budget method when applicable;
- measurement/KPIs;
- relevant market, sector, or seasonal context.

### Hard filters

Every query requires:

- `review_status = approved`;
- current time within effective/expiry bounds;
- compatible locale/market rules;
- compatible knowledge kind and task category.

Market preference is Egypt, then MENA, then global. A fallback must be labeled;
global guidance is never silently presented as Egyptian evidence.

### Candidate selection

For each category:

1. retrieve up to 12 semantic candidates after metadata filtering;
2. remove duplicate chunks and near-duplicate source versions;
3. keep at most two chunks from the same entry;
4. choose up to eight total chunks across the required categories;
5. hydrate canonical text, citations, and validity from PostgreSQL.

Retrieval scores rank candidates but do not create evidence quality. Evidence
tier and source governance remain separate metadata.

### Missing knowledge

If a required category has no approved useful result, return a visible knowledge
gap. The Strategy may omit a non-critical detail, create an assumption, or block
approval. It must not replace missing knowledge with unsupported model memory.

## 12. Retrieved Knowledge Pack

The retrieval service returns a structured `RetrievedKnowledgePack`:

```text
retrieval_run_id
query_summary
profile_version_id
brief_id
items[]:
  chunk_id
  entry_id/version
  title and excerpt
  kind and tags
  relevance score
  evidence tier
  source references
  effective/expiry dates
knowledge_gaps[]
embedding and retrieval configuration
```

The full pack is persisted so the team can reproduce why a Strategy version was
generated from those sources.

## 13. Channel scoring

The channel score is deterministic. The model explains it but cannot change the
numbers.

Dimensions:

- objective fit;
- audience fit;
- existing presence;
- asset/format fit;
- team capacity;
- budget fit;
- evidence strength;
- measurement readiness.

The result selects no more than two primary channels and one supporting channel.
Paid tactics are excluded when `paid_media_allowed` is false. A channel can be
rejected despite popularity when capacity or measurement readiness is too weak.

## 14. Budget and KPI policy

Budget means external marketing spend only. It excludes MarketMind subscription
pricing and never authorizes real spending.

Supported target modes:

- `establish_baseline`;
- `owner_target`;
- `baseline_improvement`;
- `verified_benchmark_range`.

Numeric benchmark ranges require a retrieved `verified_benchmark` entry that is
current, compatible, and cited. Assumptions cannot be formatted as benchmarks.
Channel allocations must equal the approved external-spend total.

## 15. Runtime and ownership

### Next.js Web

- Strategy Brief, clarification, generation progress, and review UI;
- evidence drawer showing retrieved knowledge and source quality;
- assumptions, gaps, blockers, profile-version staleness, and decision controls;
- owner approve, revise, or reject actions.

### NestJS API

- authentication, RBAC, ownership, and journey state;
- profile/brief readiness and idempotency;
- Strategy persistence, immutable versions, and owner decisions;
- BullMQ `strategy-generation` orchestration and retries;
- public API and FastAPI internal calls.

### FastAPI AI service

- knowledge ingestion and embedding adapters;
- Qdrant retrieval and PostgreSQL hydration contract;
- marketing methodology and deterministic channel scoring;
- structured generation prompts and response schemas;
- citation, leakage, arithmetic, and section validation;
- mock providers for deterministic tests.

### PostgreSQL, Redis, and Qdrant

- PostgreSQL: authoritative profiles, briefs, knowledge, chunks, retrieval runs,
  Strategy versions, sources, and decisions;
- Redis/BullMQ: asynchronous Strategy generation;
- Qdrant: rebuildable vectors and filterable chunk payloads.

### Research Agent compatibility

Curated RAG is shared infrastructure, not a sixth agent. The future Research
Agent may use it and add a cited `ResearchPack` from limited trusted research.
Strategy v1 can run without that future pack, but it cannot invent fresh market
or competitor facts when the profile and curated library do not contain them.

- `POST /api/v1/strategies`

- `POST /api/v1/strategies/:id/generate`
- `GET /api/v1/strategies/:id`

- `POST /api/v1/strategies/:id/decisions`
- `POST /api/v1/strategies/:id/retry`
- `GET /api/v1/journey/current`

### Internal AI API

- `POST /internal/v1/ai/knowledge/retrieve`
- `POST /internal/v1/ai/strategy/generate`
- `POST /internal/v1/ai/strategy/revise`

Initial ingestion is an authenticated/idempotent CLI used by the team, not a
public upload endpoint. A knowledge administration UI is deferred.

### Lifecycle

```text
needs_brief -> ready -> retrieving -> queued -> generating -> validating
             -> draft -> approved | rejected | failed
```

Revision creates a new immutable Strategy version and a new retrieval run. A
failed revision never destroys the prior draft.

## 17. Validation and failure handling

Reject or block plans that:

- use an unconfirmed or stale profile without acknowledgement;
- exceed the 2+1 channel limit;
- include paid tactics when paid is disallowed;
- exceed budget or capacity;
- contain incorrect allocation arithmetic;
- cite chunks not present in the persisted retrieval pack;
- cite retired, expired, or unapproved knowledge;
- present assumptions as confirmed facts;
- contain a numeric benchmark without verified evidence;
- leak captions, finished posts, assets, publishing, or ad execution;
- omit required Strategy sections.

Qdrant failure is retryable. If retrieval cannot complete, generation does not
silently fall back to model memory. Existing approved Strategy versions remain
available.

## 18. RAG evaluation and acceptance

The first evaluation set contains at least 25 reviewed cases covering retail,
hospitality, services, education, and healthcare in Arabic, English, and mixed
language. Cases vary objective, budget mode, channel availability, capacity,
missing knowledge, expired knowledge, and conflicting tags.

Minimum acceptance:

- expected relevant source appears in the top five for at least 80% of the
  curated evaluation queries; this is an internal engineering target;
- no unapproved or expired result passes hard filters;
- no incompatible hard-sector or paid-media result passes its filter;
- 100% of numeric benchmark claims cite a retrieved verified benchmark;
- every citation resolves to the persisted retrieval pack and PostgreSQL source;
- retrieval failure and empty results are visible and do not produce unsupported
  claims;
- no Business Profile is stored in the shared Qdrant collection;
- comparison cases show the RAG plan is more grounded and traceable than the
  same generation without retrieved knowledge.

Track retrieval latency, embedding cost, top-k hit rate, empty-result rate,
knowledge gaps, citation validation failures, approval rate, and revision rate.
